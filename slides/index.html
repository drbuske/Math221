Motivating Questions

Definition 1.

For an object moving according to position function \(s(t)\text{,}\) the average velocity on the interval \([a,a+h]\) is given by

\begin{equation*} AV_{[a,a+h]}= \frac{s(a+h)-s(a)}{h}. \end{equation*}

With the notion of a limit, we can now understand instantaneous velocity.

Definition 2.

The instantaneous velocity at \(a\) is the limit of average velocity \(AV_{[a,a+h]}\) as \(h \rightarrow 0\text{.}\) That is,

\begin{equation*} IV_{t=a}= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0}AV_{[a,a+h]}= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{s(a+h)-s(a)}{h}. \end{equation*}

The Derivative of a Function at a Point

We want to find how the volume of a balloon changes as it is filled with air. We know \(\displaystyle V(r) = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^{3}\text{,}\) where \(r\) is the radius in inches and \(V(r)\) is the volume is in cubic inches. The expression \(\displaystyle \frac{V(3)-V(1)}{3-1}\) represents the average rate of change of

  1. the radius with respect to the volume when the radius changes from 1 inch to 3 inches.

  2. the radius with respect to the volume when the volume changes from 1 cubic inch to 3 cubic inches.

  3. the volume with respect to the radius when the radius changes from 1 inch to 3 inches.

  4. the volume with respect to the radius when the volume changes from 1 cubic inch to 3 cubic inches.
