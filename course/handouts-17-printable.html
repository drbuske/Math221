    Section Overview

    We begin our work in Chapter 3 (Using the Derivative) by seeing how the derivative may be employed to relate the rates of two different quantities that are related and each changing as time varies. The main idea here is: if two quantities are related to one another, and each is changing as time changes, then the rates at which each quantity is changing must be related. Hence, we consider a class of problems known as related rates problems.

    Subsection After class

    1. We know that \(dV/dt = 10\) where \(V\) represents the volume of water in the tank at time \(t\text{.}\) Since \(V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r(t)^{2} h(t)\) where \(\frac{r(t)}{h(t)}= \frac{4}{10}\) we have that
      \begin{equation*} V(t) = \frac{1}{3}\pi \left( \frac{2}{5}h(t) \right)^{2} h(t) = \frac{4}{75}\pi h(t)^{3}. \end{equation*}
      So, \(\displaystyle \frac{dV}{dt}= \frac{4}{75}\pi \cdot 3 h(t)^{2} \frac{dh}{dt}\text{.}\) We solve for \(\frac{dh}{dt}\) and evaluate for \(h=5\) feet. But
      \begin{equation*} \frac{dh}{dt}|_{h=5}= \frac{\frac{dV}{dt}}{\frac{12}{75}\pi (5^{2})}= \frac{10}{4\pi}= \frac{5}{2\pi}\ {\textrm{ft/min}}. \end{equation*}
