    Handout Daily Prep 1.6 - The Second Derivative
    

    Section Overview

    We have spent a lot of time so far focusing on the derivative of a function, which we will in the future also call the ‚Äúfirst derivative‚Äù: its definition, its meaning, and various interpretations in applied contexts. The first derivative, in essence, tells us whether a given function is increasing or decreasing at a certain point. Next, we are interested in learning how a function is increasing or decreasing at a given point, and the key tool to doing so is the second derivative of the function: Here, we consider what happens when we consider the derivative of the derivative of a function.

    • Do a computation in which you find \(f''(x)\) for \(f(x) = 5x^{2}\) two ways. First, you already watched a video on algebraically computing a second derivative using a limit: Limit Definition of the Second Derivative (9:48). Similar to this video, compute \(f''(x)\text{.}\) There is, however, a single limit that can be computed to determine \(f''\text{.}\) For any point \(a\text{,}\) it turns out that
      \begin{equation*} f''(a) = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{f(a+2h)-2f(a+h)+f(a)}{h^{2}}. \end{equation*}
      Use this formula to compute \(f''(x)\) when \(f(x) = 5x^{2}\) and compare to the answer you found using the method from the video.
